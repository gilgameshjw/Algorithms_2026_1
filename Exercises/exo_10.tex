\begin{problem}[Cut Property Proof, 5 pts]
Prove by induction on the number of edges that for any cut $(S, V \setminus S)$ in a connected weighted graph $G = (V, E, w)$, the minimum-weight edge crossing the cut belongs to every minimum spanning tree of $G$.
\end{problem}


\begin{problem}[Brain and Graph Algorithms, 5 pts]
Assume you are given a complete structural connectome modeled as a directed weighted graph $G = (V, E, w)$, where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $V$ represents neuronal populations or brain regions,
    \item $(u, v) \in E$ represents a directed synaptic/axonal connection,
    \item $w(u, v) > 0$ encodes transmission delay or metabolic cost.
\end{itemize}
You aim to retro-engineer distinct ``thinking flows'' (information propagation patterns) that underlie cognitive processes. For each phenomenon below, specify the most appropriate graph algorithm(s), justify your choice, and state the expected time complexity:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(a)] \textbf{Hierarchical feedforward cascades} (e.g., sensory or motor pathways where information flows strictly forward without loops).
    \item[(b)] \textbf{Recurrent feedback circuits} (e.g., working memory, sustained attention, or oscillatory activity that circulates within a closed subset of regions).
    \item[(c)] \textbf{Fastest reactive pathways} (e.g., rapid decision-making or reflex-like responses that minimize delay from stimulus to action).
\end{enumerate}
Finally, state one fundamental limitation of applying static graph algorithms to model real neural ``thinking flows''.
\end{problem}
